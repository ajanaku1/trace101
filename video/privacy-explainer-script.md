# Trace Privacy Explainer Video Script
## "Your Wallet Has a Shadow" (2:30)

---

### OPENING (0:00 - 0:20)

**VISUAL:** Dark screen. A single glowing wallet address appears. Then lines start connecting to it—transactions, other wallets, slowly building into a web.

**NARRATION:**
"Every time you make a trade on a public blockchain, you leave a trace. Not just a receipt—a permanent, public record that anyone can see. Forever."

---

### THE PROBLEM (0:20 - 0:55)

**VISUAL:** The web expands. Labels appear: "Exchange withdrawal," "NFT purchase," "Friend's wallet," "Twitter post." The wallet is now at the center of a surveillance map.

**NARRATION:**
"That one time you posted your wallet on Twitter? Now your entire financial history is linked to your name. That transfer to your other wallet? They're clustered together in surveillance databases. Companies like Chainalysis and Arkham don't just see your transactions—they build profiles. Your timezone from when you trade. Your wealth from your balances. Your social circle from who you transact with."

**VISUAL:** A profile card appears with silhouette, showing "Active hours: 9am-11pm EST," "Net worth estimate," "Risk score," "Connected wallets: 12"

---

### WHY IT MATTERS (0:55 - 1:25)

**VISUAL:** Split screen showing three scenarios with simple icons/animations

**NARRATION:**
"So what? You're not doing anything wrong.

Here's the thing: visibility creates vulnerability.

When traders see your wallet buying a token, they front-run you—buying first, driving up the price before your order fills.

When scammers see your balance, you become a target for phishing attacks tailored specifically to you.

And when everyone can copy your trades in real-time, any edge you have disappears."

**VISUAL:** Show: (1) Bot sandwich attacking a transaction (2) Phishing email with accurate balance details (3) Copy-traders mirroring moves

---

### THE SOLUTION (1:25 - 2:00)

**VISUAL:** The tangled web from before. A shield appears, and selective lines break—not everything, just specific connections.

**NARRATION:**
"The answer isn't total anonymity—that raises red flags and compliance issues. The answer is selective privacy.

Break the link between your known identity and specific activities. Keep your main wallet clean and transparent. Use private execution for trades you don't want front-run or copied.

This is what encrypt.trade does. It lets you execute trades without broadcasting them to the entire network first. No mempool visibility. No copy-traders. No sandwich bots. Just your trade, executed privately."

**VISUAL:** Simple animation showing a trade going through a shield/lock, arriving at destination without the surveillance web seeing it

---

### CALL TO ACTION (2:00 - 2:30)

**VISUAL:** Trace logo appears. Then encrypt.trade logo.

**NARRATION:**
"Want to know how exposed you already are? Trace analyzes your wallet and shows you exactly what surveillance platforms can see—your risk score, your connected wallets, your behavioral patterns.

Then, when you're ready to trade privately, encrypt.trade is there.

Your money. Your trades. Your privacy."

**VISUAL:**
- trace-wheat.vercel.app
- encrypt.trade
- Tagline: "See your exposure. Take back control."

---

## Production Notes

**Tone:** Calm, informative, slightly urgent but not alarmist. Like a knowledgeable friend explaining something important.

**Music:** Subtle electronic ambient, builds slightly during "the problem" section, resolves during solution.

**Visual Style:** Dark theme with purple/indigo accents (matching Trace brand). Clean motion graphics, no stock footage of "hackers in hoodies."

**Duration:** 2 minutes 30 seconds

**Key Messages:**
1. Public blockchains are surveillance systems by default
2. Visibility creates real risks (front-running, targeting, copy-trading)
3. Selective privacy > total anonymity
4. Trace shows your exposure, encrypt.trade lets you trade privately
